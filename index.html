Our Story

Born From Devotion
and Duty

Shree Gau Dham Gaushala was founded in 2006 by a small circle of devoted volunteers who could not bear to see the sacred cow abandoned or left to suffer. What began with a handful of rescued animals has grown into a 12-acre sanctuary that protects over 1,200 cows daily.

We operate on the goodwill and generosity of our community. Every rupee donated goes directly to feed, medicine, and the care of our sacred residents.

🌟

Our Vision

A world where no cow is abandoned, hungry, or left without care.

🎯

Our Mission

To shelter, heal, nourish, and dignify every cow that comes to us.

Ancient Wisdom, Living Practice

Why Gau Seva Matters

The cow has been the cornerstone of Indian civilisation for millennia — spiritually, agriculturally, and ecologically.

01

Spiritual Significance

In Vedic tradition, the cow embodies abundance and compassion. Serving Gaumata is considered one of the highest forms of seva, earning divine blessings for the giver and their family.

02

Ecological Balance

Cow dung and urine are the foundation of organic farming. Panchagavya enriches soil, replaces harmful chemicals, and supports sustainable agriculture across our region.

03

Community Livelihood

Our gaushala supports local farmers with organic inputs, creates employment for caretakers, and provides a living example of the ancient human-cow bond.

04

Protecting the Helpless

Thousands of cows roam city streets, injured and starving. Our shelter gives them dignity — from abandoned calves to old cows who can no longer fend for themselves.

05

Healing & Medicine

Gau-based products like ghee, ark, and gobar dye have been used in Ayurveda for thousands of years. Our cows contribute to wellness traditions that heal millions.

06

Cultural Continuity

Gaushala seva keeps alive a 5,000-year-old cultural tradition. Every child who visits learns respect for nature, animals, and the ancient values of Bharat.
